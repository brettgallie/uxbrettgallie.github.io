Robotics and Voice UX

1
The UX Challenge

Artemis is going to be a massive project with many working parts. I wanted to build a ‘proof of concept’ that could be used to get the look and feel of a fully functioning Lunar Gateway with its own onboard robot. One of the functions of R2D2 in the Star Wars films is holographic video playback of messages. With our current technology, I have simulated this function by instructing my Robot prototype to playback a NASA Youtube Video using Voice Commands to Alexa to turn on an IOT smart plug that activates my mini-video -projector. This would be one of the many functions that could be built - in on the Artemis Robot Companion.

2
The UX Approach

I experimented with a range of voice activated tech and used the Adobe Voice UX modules to built out the prototype. Ultimately the robot is a cloud based robot intended to be used on the Lunar Gateway supporting the Astronauts in their tasks. The robot is a hybrid combining the best of all previous ISS robot experiments (eg. Astrobee, Astro-CIMON, NASA-REALMS) and incorporates the body of R2-D2 (with its multi-tools) and the Cloud Brain of an Alexa device.

3
The Final product

The voice ux commands have been tested out at the hackathon with the prototype robot.

4
The UX Impact

Amazon are working on a home Alexa based robot which in the future could be adapted for onboard use on the ISS, Lunar Gateway.
